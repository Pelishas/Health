📚 Educational Resources

First Trimester (Weeks 1-12)

🌱 What to Expect

Morning sickness, fatigue, and breast tenderness are common. Your baby's major organs are developing.

🍎 Nutrition Tips

Take prenatal vitamins, eat folate-rich foods, avoid alcohol and raw foods. Stay hydrated.

⚠️ Warning Signs

Severe cramping, heavy bleeding, severe nausea/vomiting. Contact your doctor immediately.

📋 Appointments

First prenatal visit, blood tests, genetic screening discussions, dating ultrasound.

Second Trimester (Weeks 13-27)

🌟 What to Expect

Energy returns, baby bump shows, you may feel baby's first movements around week 18-20.

🥗 Nutrition Focus

Increase calcium and iron intake. Continue prenatal vitamins. Eat protein-rich foods.

🏃♀️ Exercise

Safe exercises include walking, swimming, prenatal yoga. Avoid contact sports.

🔍 Tests & Scans

Anatomy scan (18-22 weeks), glucose screening, blood pressure monitoring.

Third Trimester (Weeks 28-40+)

🤰 What to Expect

Baby grows rapidly, you may experience back pain, shortness of breath, and frequent urination.

🎒 Birth Preparation

Pack hospital bag, create birth plan, attend childbirth classes, choose pediatrician.

⚡ Labor Signs

Regular contractions, water breaking, bloody show, back pain. Know when to go to hospital.

👶 Final Preparations

Baby's room setup, car seat installation, newborn care basics, breastfeeding preparation.

⚠️ When to Seek Immediate Care

🩸 Bleeding & Pain

  • Heavy vaginal bleeding
  • Severe abdominal or pelvic pain
  • Severe cramping
  • Signs of miscarriage

🤒 Severe Symptoms

  • High fever (over 101°F)
  • Severe headaches
  • Vision changes or blurred vision
  • Severe swelling of face/hands

👶 Baby-Related Concerns

  • No fetal movement for 12+ hours
  • Decreased fetal movement
  • Fluid leaking (possible water breaking)
  • Regular contractions before 37 weeks

🫁 Breathing & Heart

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Chest pain
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Fainting or dizziness
